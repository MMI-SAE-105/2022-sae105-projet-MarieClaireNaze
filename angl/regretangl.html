Regret: A White Rabbit life

Mana Thoughts:

Our generation was special, it was called "GenZ", like the last letter of the alphabet, the last generation of an era.

"What will you be later?"

When we were children, the most common question was "what will you do when you grow up? We imagined ourselves as doctors, teachers or even singers!

Then we started to run after time, without even realising it, so we got used to it very quickly.

To know if our life was not a failure, we all compared ourselves without realising that we were only trying to feel superior to others. Yet as the song "Grow up" by stray kids says, we each have to take our own time.

This race against the clock was everywhere, you always had to be the first one to have passed your studies, to have a job, a family life and children, to fulfil the good little scheme of life drawn by society.

"Nothing of value is lost by taking your time"

Therefore, "What are you going to do later?" which was an innocent question at first, became a knife to the throat, an unbearable pressure for anyone who did not know what to do with his life. And when you found yourself saying "Later, I don't have time", yet as Abraham Lincoln said, "Nothing of value is lost by taking your time", which is why it is important to take the time to live your life.

In the end, it was only in retirement that one could hope to truly live one's life, where one no longer owed anything to anyone.

At least that's what we saw when we looked at our parents and we didn't want to end up like that.

"TIME LOOP"
