DR STRANGE: The Time Loop’s paradox

A fantastic movie…

Dr Strange is a fantasy and action film by director and writer Scott Derrickson released in 2016 by Walt disney motion pictures.

It is part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe, the film adaptation of Marvel comics created in the 1930s. This film is the first of three installments to date that features Stan Lee and Steve Ditko's Dr. Strange comics.

It stars Benedict Cumberbatch as Dr. Stephen Strange, Mads Mikkelsen as the antagonist Kaecilius, Tilda Swinton as the former, and a cameo by Stan Lee, who has appeared in every Marvel film.

Poster of the movie "Dr Strange"

... and a story that is just as much

The film follows the story of Doctor Stephen Strange, a brilliant but pretentious man who excels in neurosurgery until he suffers a serious accident that prevents him from practicing his profession.

After hearing the testimony of a former paraplegic, Strange decides to give up everything to go in search of Kamar-Taj in the heart of Tibet.

Stephen Strange will then discover the Ancient One, who leads an order of wizards, where he will discover a form of magic linked to metaphysics and alternative universes. He will have to learn to put his pride aside to wield this power and save the world from an immortal entity called Dormammu.

Eye for going back in time

time loops paradox

One of the most important themes in Dr Strange is time: it takes time for Stephen to accept his condition, it takes time for him to find Kamar-Taj, it takes time for him to learn to use magic.

But the facet of time that will interest us here is the time loop.

A time loop is a form of time travel, in fiction, a character will be able to go back in time by a few seconds as in Dr Strange or by several years as in Back to the Future. The protagonist will therefore be able to influence the course of history at will. This can sometimes create a paradox: an event that was supposed to happen has been prevented, it causes an alteration of the story.

"I can lose again and again that which makes you my prisoner"

In the climax of the film it is this power that Dr Strange uses: He locks himself and Dormammu in a time loop where he attempts to negotiate the safety of the Earth only to be killed by his enemy each time. His power allows him to come back to life and start over where he failed, so he influences the course of things.

Thus we see that the time loop prevents the fear of failure, a fear well known to our generations, but it could also be the key to one of our greatest hopes: to have a world in our image.
